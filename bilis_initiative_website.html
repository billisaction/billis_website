Who we are

A community that believes in every girl's right to be whole

Bilis Initiative is a community-driven organisation dedicated to ending Female Genital Mutilation and advancing the rights, dignity, and empowerment of women and girls in Somali communities.

We don't work from the outside in. We work from the inside out — with cultural understanding, survivor voices, and deep-rooted trust built over years of relationship.

Because lasting change doesn't happen through pressure. It happens through belonging, conversation, and courage.

Survivor-centred approach

Every programme, every decision, starts with those who have lived experience. Their voice is our compass.

Cultural understanding

We engage communities from within — using language, relationships, and respect to create genuine dialogue.

Transforming social norms

Awareness alone doesn't change behaviour. We work on the beliefs and conversations that drive lasting transformation.

Safe environments for girls

We build support systems — in schools, families, and communities — where girls can grow up protected and empowered.

What we do

Our programmes

Education

Girl Power Programme

Equipping adolescent girls with life skills, knowledge of their rights, and the confidence to speak up — in school, at home, and in their communities.

Engagement

Community & Family Dialogue

Bringing together families, religious leaders, and men to open honest conversations about FGM — and to build shared commitments to end it.

Advocacy

Policy & Legal Advocacy

Pushing for the enforcement of laws and policies against FGM, and amplifying survivor voices at local, national, and international levels.

Our objectives

Five pillars of change

01

Awareness & behaviour change through media

Using powerful media to shift public understanding and drive meaningful change in attitudes toward FGM.

02

Adolescent girl empowerment

Giving girls knowledge, life skills, and the confidence to know and demand their rights.

03

Family & community engagement

Involving families, religious leaders, and men as partners in protection — online and offline.

04

Protection & referral systems

Strengthening community-based networks so girls at risk have safe paths to support and safety.

05

Legal & policy advocacy

Advocating for laws to be enforced, and ensuring that protection on paper becomes protection in practice.
